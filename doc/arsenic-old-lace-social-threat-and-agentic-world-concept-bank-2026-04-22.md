# Arsenic-and-Old-Lace social threat and agentic-world concept bank (2026-04-22)

Status: checkpointed far-back parked concept substrate.

## Intent

Preserve the current outside-the-box C-AOL concept bank without pretending any of it is active implementation work now.
This bank exists so later planning does not lose the good ideas or rediscover them from scratch.
It is explicitly subordinate to honest playtesting and cleanup of the current bandit and Basecamp zoning footing.

## Classification

- **State:** PARKED / FAR-BACK CONCEPT BANK
- **Queue position:** not active, not greenlit backlog, not `TODO.md` material
- **Promotion rule:** unpack one bounded item at a time only after current bandit and Basecamp zoning play is tested honestly enough that new concept work will not pile onto mush

## Nearer playable threat/control seeds

These are still parked, but they read like the most believable future bounded promotions.

### Camp alarm states with natural-language control

Keep the expansion narrow and readable:
- camps can switch between a few explicit alert/posture states
- those states should be controllable through natural language, especially yelled commands
- examples include things like `everyone inside`, `to positions`, `lights out`, `stay sharp`, and `stand down`
- the response layer should keep NPC personality instead of sounding like menu sludge

### Speech as command bandwidth

Treat whisper / normal / yell and similar organic player speech as a bounded tactical/social channel rather than as a fake general AI promise.
This is about believable command/control feel, not about making camp parse every sentence under the sun.

### Bandits exploiting legible camp routines

If camps become more readable, hostile actors should be able to exploit:
- patrol gaps
- light signatures
- repeated route habits
- known calm windows
- false-distress or false-safety lures

### Radio information warfare

Radios should matter much more than they currently do.
Possible uses:
- real distress calls
- fake distress calls / trap bait
- bandit chatter or intercepted movement hints
- camp warnings or help requests
- secret psychopath / cannibal-clan communications

### Writhing stalker

A single strong zombie-like predator that shadows the player and attacks when the player is already in a bad situation, especially while occupied with zombies.
The point is predation and timing, not fair stand-up combat.

### Sight avoidance for stalking actors

While in stalking mode and inside the reality bubble, these enemies should prefer not to remain on tiles visible to the player or nearby NPCs.
Keep it bounded:
- prefer leaving seen tiles
- use current exposure / recent exposure heuristics
- do not give magical perfect future-cone omniscience

### Feral camp

A rougher hostile-camp pressure model than the current bandit shape.
Possible distinguishing traits:
- lower interest in ordinary NPC threat calculations
- more sleep-opportunist raiding
- uglier herd logic
- less strategic discipline, more savage timing

## Arsenic-and-Old-Lace social horror bank

This family is about the tone Josef explicitly wants: nice-seeming people, hidden rot, social reading, and the possibility that your fellow survivors are badly wrong in the head.

### Social camouflage

Some apparently normal survivors should be dangerous in ways that are not obvious on first meeting.
The tension comes from reading them, talking to them, and slowly spotting what is wrong.

### Friendly psychos and secret predatory affiliations

Examples to preserve:
- secret psychopaths hiding inside otherwise normal survivor spaces
- cannibal-clan or similarly rotten affiliations hidden behind ordinary speech
- apparently legitimate radio traffic that reveals something much worse if you keep listening

### Faction mythmaking and rumor ecology

The world should be able to talk about the player, other camps, raids, rescues, atrocities, or weird phenomena in ways that are not just objective fact dumps.
A deterministic event base can still feed varied rumors, exaggerations, fear, and folklore.

### Living camp mood and fracture

Camps should eventually feel like groups of people under pressure, not just labor menus.
Preserve the concept of readable camp weather such as fear, resentment, zeal, suspicion, grief, or brittle optimism.

### Survivor procedural culture

Different survivor groups should be able to carry their own slang, habits, taboos, and local worldview instead of all sounding like the same cardboard voice in different jackets.

## Big weird-world systemic swings

These are farther out, but worth preserving because they actually fit Cataclysm's absurdly broad setting.

### Conspiracy pressure system

Government black-project residue, terminals, radio fragments, labs, and survivor testimony should create an evolving paranoia layer where people form beliefs, not just consume exposition.

### Multi-species territorial intelligence

Zombies, fungus, triffids, ferals, bandits, camps, and stranger actors should sometimes create competing local ecologies and exploitable pressure interactions instead of behaving like isolated spawn tables.

### Dream / signal intrusion

Rare but meaningful weird-pressure events tied to dreams, radios, terminals, artifacts, or other reality-tearing sources.
The point is not constant hallucination sludge, but disturbing information pressure that changes how the player reads the world.

### Agentic NPCs with their own goals

NPCs should eventually be able to pursue ambitions that are not reducible to `follow player, give quest, wait in mapgen box`.
The strongest preserved hook here is the currently underused interdimensional-traveler footing.
Possible future shape:
- traveler NPCs with large motives or obscure missions
- goals that intersect the player without revolving entirely around the player
- questlines or social entanglements that feel grand enough to justify their strange origin

### Apocalypse authorship tools

Preserve the idea of bounded incident/quest/social packet generation rather than infinite slurry.
The useful version is small, inspectable, stateful local narrative packets, not a fake forever-story engine.

## Preservation notes

The strongest current cross-links in this bank are:
- alarm states + natural-language yelling
- readable camp routines + bandit exploitation
- radios as information warfare / trap / social-horror channel
- stalker predators + bounded sight avoidance
- hidden-psychopath social camouflage as the clearest `Arsenic and Old Lace` tone expression
- agentic NPC goals as the broadest long-range world-expansion family

## Non-goals

This bank does **not**:
- greenlight implementation now
- widen the current active work stack
- excuse skipping proper bandit/Basecamp zoning playtesting
- claim the current tree already supports these systems
- turn `Plan.md` into a graveyard of fifty speculative micro-items

## If this bank is revisited later

Do not reopen it as fog.
Promote one bounded item at a time.
Good early candidates, if later evidence supports them, would be things like:
- alarm states with yelled natural-language control
- bandits exploiting camp routines / radio bait
- writhing stalker + bounded sight avoidance
- a first social-camouflage packet

If a future promotion cannot be named cleanly and scoped narrowly, it should stay parked.
