## Preserved Josef text

> A slim, shaking figure. Grey arms wrapped around it's chest, hugging it's twitching and writhing body. It's eyes make your neck hair stand: half crazed, half panicked, they steer around in prehensile madness. It's the eyes that do it for you, not it's dripping teeth.

## Starting product picture

A rare first-generation zombie-adjacent singleton predator: not a camp, faction, Basecamp economy actor, or bandit profile with a different hat.

It should feel like one wrong thing moving through the same overmap ecology as bandits. It is drawn by human/player evidence, night light, town/building edges, forest cover, route pressure, and zombie-distraction opportunities.

## Seed behavior shape

- wander through coarse overmap interest: town/building edges, forest cover, roads/city margins, and occasional night light marks;
- ignore smoke or treat it as weak/indirect information rather than food/prey;
- latch onto believable player/human clues with a leash, not permanent omniscience;
- approach indirectly and prefer cover/darkness/clutter;
- attack when the player is distracted, hurt, bleeding, low stamina, noisy, or engaged with zombies;
- strike quickly with cut/bleed pressure, then withdraw when hurt/exposed/focused;
- re-latch only if the player keeps feeding it signals.

## Substrate note

The current bandit structural-bounty work should not be copied wholesale. Bandits interpret overmap interest as bounty/risk/manpower economy; a writhing stalker would interpret it as scent/appetite/fixation/opportunity.

If promoted later, this probably wants a shared lower-level overmap-interest substrate, then a singleton hostile actor/latch layer on top. It should not inherit bandit harvesting, camp reserves, shakedown logic, or faction economics.
