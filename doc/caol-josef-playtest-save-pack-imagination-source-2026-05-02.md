# CAOL-JOSEF-PLAYTEST-SAVE-PACK-v0 imagination source (2026-05-02)

Josef wants to playtest the whole current C-AOL mess as a living set of scenes, not as a stack of proof receipts. The finished thing is a small, labelled pack of current-build saves or handoff sessions that he can load, understand quickly, and poke for product taste.

## Finished scene in the head

Josef opens a folder or card of prepared playtest entries. Each entry has a plain label, a short “what to try” note, a known-good current-build load/start state, and a warning about what is staged versus natural. He can pick one scene at a time: Basecamp AI/camp locker usefulness, bandit pressure, cannibal camp pressure, flesh raptor skirmishing, zombie rider terror/counterplay, and writhing stalker hit-fade dread.

He should not need to inspect harness logs, remember debug commands, or reverse-engineer which old run matters. The saves should feel like postcards from the current product: small enough to play in minutes, clear enough to judge, and honest enough that staged setup does not pretend to be natural discovery.

## Taste questions Josef should be able to answer

- Does the scene load cleanly and explain itself before he starts pressing keys?
- Does the intended behavior show up quickly enough to judge?
- Does the scene feel alive, fair, readable, optically legible, and fun?
- Does the scene carry the C-AOL gnostic/occult wrongness where appropriate, or does it feel like ordinary mechanics in a costume?
- Is the counterplay or usefulness visible from play, not just from logs?
- Does anything feel too staged, fake, spammy, unfair, invisible, or annoying?
- Which of these scenes deserves a follow-up fix or tuning packet after Josef’s taste pass?

## Required save/postcard set

V0 should keep the old route labels visible — `/visions/camp-locker`, `/rider/stalker/raptor`, and `/horde/camp` — while preparing labelled entries for:

1. **Basecamp AI / camp locker usefulness** — followers, camp board/assignment/usefulness, locker-zone stock, practical equipment service.
2. **Bandit pressure / shakedown / basecamp contact** — living-world risk around camp or nearby owned-site pressure, including scenic shakedown where useful.
3. **Cannibal camp pressure** — hostile camp pressure/smoke/local-contact footing from the cannibal rows.
4. **Flesh raptor** — circling/skirmisher behavior and annoyance-vs-fun comparison.
5. **Zombie rider** — late-world mounted predator pressure, bow/counterplay, camp-light/no-light or open-field pressure as appropriate.
6. **Writhing stalker** — current hit-fade retreat rhythm and/or zombie-shadow pressure with clean caveats.

## Thematic contrast Josef wants before playtest

The pack should include paired contrast rows where possible, because these scenes need to feel like different instincts rather than one canned monster/bandit trick:

- Stalker, no fire / low threat: closes, whacks, fades out.
- Stalker, fire/light present: keeps stalking or hesitates, not a dumb charge into the obvious danger.
- Stalker, high threat/support: stalks/pressures rather than suiciding.
- Bandits, no obvious signal: no instant theater.
- Bandits, fire/smoke/basecamp signal: visible scouting, shakedown, hold-off, or camp pressure.
- Bandits, high-threat/resistant setup: stalking, holding off, backing out, or different escalation instead of the same demand loop.

## What must be true

- Every entry has a stable label and path/command the user can run or load.
- Every entry has a 2-5 minute play instruction and 2-4 focused taste questions.
- Every entry has a current-build load/startup check before being handed to Josef.
- Portal-storm contamination is surfaced using the harness warning light and either avoided or explicitly allowed.
- Existing green proof rows are used as footing, but they are not laundered into fresh natural-discovery claims.
- Dirty or blocked rows, such as old blocked e2e routes, are either repaired with a new handoff/save or excluded with a caveat.
- If a scene cannot be made cleanly playable, it becomes an explicit caveated card rather than a fake green save.

## Non-goals

- Do not implement new gameplay unless save prep exposes a hard blocker that prevents the playtest from existing.
- Do not reopen every closed v0 lane just because it appears in the pack.
- Do not create release packaging.
- Do not turn this into broad balancing or a hidden tuning pass.
- Do not make Josef prove agent claims; his job here is product judgment.

## Failure smells

- A save loads, but Josef has no idea what to try.
- The card says “bandit pressure” but the save needs ten minutes of invisible waiting before anything happens.
- The pack points at stale binaries, dirty portal-storm contamination, or old screenshots instead of current playable sessions.
- A staged setup is described like natural random discovery.
- The pack includes six scenes, but only two are actually playable without archaeology.

## Review stance

This lane succeeds when Josef receives a usable playtest save pack with honest caveats. It does not succeed merely because old proof docs exist. The proof docs are footing; the product here is the playable handoff.
