# Zombie rider 0.3 imagination source of truth — 2026-04-30

## Preserved flavor text

This exact text is product canon. Preserve punctuation unless Josef explicitly revises it.

```text
Up on, what can only be described as, a six legged horse - or is it a spider? - a towering figure, with eyes the color of blood, holds a gory bow of wet bones and sinews.
It's movement ferocious, as the tumbling feet hasten across the terrain.
Running is out of the question.
```

## Finished scene

The zombie rider is release 0.3’s endpoint cavalry horror: the mature-world mutation that makes the player afraid of open ground, roads, long sightlines, and careless camp light. It is not a fast zombie with a bow stapled to the JSON. It is a mounted late-game predator: blood-eyed rider, six-legged horse-spider body, wet bone-and-sinew bow, too fast for ordinary flight, too mobile to treat like a turret.

The player should understand three facts very quickly:

1. running across open ground is no longer a plan;
2. cover, narrow passages, doors, terrain, darkness, smoke, and line-of-sight discipline matter;
3. late-game light can call trouble from the wider map.

Its body should do useful design work. If the rider is mechanically large/huge, existing small-passage rules can make windows, embrasures, and tight openings matter without inventing a fresh movement limiter. But this must be tested honestly: normal doors and open corridors may still be passable, so “tall” only counts when the engine actually blocks the tile.

A lone rider is a roaming mounted skirmisher. It shoots, repositions, withdraws from bad odds, returns if advantage persists, and harasses instead of politely closing to die. Two riders meeting is worse: they should be able to form a rider band, turning a local scare into a circling late-game map problem.

## Endpoint mutation feel

The rider belongs near the end of the zombie evolution line, in the same emotional tier as terminal brute-type horrors. It should not surprise a poorly equipped year-one player as routine content. Year two or equivalent mature-world evolution is the default design smell unless implementation evidence says otherwise.

This endpoint status justifies high speed, ranged harassment, overmap light pressure, and band formation. It does not justify unavoidable bullshit. Endgame danger still needs readable counterplay.

## Overmap light pressure

Late-game camp light should become a regional signal. Bright campfires, roof/elevated fire, vehicle lights, floodlights, or base lighting may attract rider attention from outside the reality bubble. If the player glows like a shopping mall in the mature apocalypse, the hills may answer.

The fun is not “camp deletion because light existed.” The fun is mounted siege/harassment pressure: the player sees consequences, can dim or relocate or fortify, and can understand why riders are circling.

## Funness bar

Fun means fair dread:

- the rider is terrifying in open space;
- the player has readable counterplay;
- shooting has rhythm and constraints, not invisible sniper tax;
- flee/shoot/flee feels cunning, not kite-lock nonsense;
- light attraction creates strategic camp tension;
- rider bands are catastrophic but capped and explainable;
- losing feels like the player ignored dangerous conditions, not like the game cheated.

## Failure smells

- Exact flavor text gets rewritten into bland grammar sludge.
- Rider appears too early to be endpoint-tier content.
- Speed makes all counterplay irrelevant.
- Ranged attack ignores cover/line of sight/terrain.
- Flee/shoot loop becomes permanent player-hostile kiting with no break condition.
- Light attraction causes instant unavoidable camp deletion.
- Rider bands accumulate forever or multiply into clown cavalry.
- Overmap AI is just local monster behavior with a bigger hat.
