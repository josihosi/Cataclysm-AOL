# Writhing stalker threat/distraction handoff — imagination source

Date: 2026-05-02
Item: `CAOL-WRITHING-STALKER-THREAT-DISTRACTION-HANDOFF-v0`

## Finished scene

The writhing stalker feels like a nervous ambush predator with memory, not a melee duelist and not a cursed garden gnome.

When the player stands with three armed friends in daylight or strong light, the stalker reads the situation as too dangerous. It does not hover at the same overmap tile, stare through the window, or offer itself to a firing squad. It melts backward into stalking distance, stays out of sight, and waits for the room to become less safe.

When night falls and the player is exposed outside, the stalker becomes dangerous again. If it can reach a good dark or covered square, it probes or strikes. If a window, wall, or door blocks it, the behavior is still legible: it circles, retreats, waits farther back, or escalates through a narrow door-opening rule. It does not stand in one bad tile all night.

The best moment is when zombies or another threat enter the player's/NPCs' tile. Then the stalker stops treating the group as calmly defended prey. It uses the chaos, chooses dark squares, cuts toward weak sightlines, and strikes during divided attention.

## Product feel

- High threat makes the stalker spooky and absent, not stupidly brave.
- Darkness and distraction make it scary again, not harmless.
- Windows and doors create readable predator choices, not indefinite loitering.
- The overmap and reality bubble feel like one creature continuing one intent.

## Failure smells

- Same OMT as three NPCs, daylight/bright light, no retreat.
- Night/outside/reachable player, no attack or reposition for many turns.
- Window-adjacent all-night hovering with no logged reason.
- Overmap state says stalking/overmatched, but bubble spawn immediately chooses close pressure.
- Bubble unload/reload resets strike budget, cooldown, or overmatched memory.
- Zombie distraction gives magical target knowledge through opaque walls instead of raising opportunity only when evidence/pathing supports it.
- Door opening turns the stalker into a clever burglar or locked-door problem solver.

## Door-opening taste line

Door opening is allowed only if it preserves the animal fantasy:

- unlocked/simple doors only;
- slow, noisy, and interruptible;
- only under darkness/opportunity/distraction after commitment;
- aborts when player/NPC threat becomes too high;
- no locked doors, no perfect house-clearing, no SWAT-goblin behavior.

If that narrow implementation is risky, defer doors and make the no-gnome reposition/retreat behavior green first.
